Gyoza

Description

Gyoza was originated from Chinese jiaozi. It was introduced to Japan during the early 20th century, but gained major popularity after World War II, when Japanese soldiers returned home with a taste for the Chinese dumplings they encountered in Manchuria.
The Japanese adapted the original recipe — making the wrappers thinner, the fillings more garlicky, and the cooking method uniquely Japanese with the famous "yaki-gyoza" (pan-fried style).

Ingredients

  • Wrapper:
    Thin flour-based dough circles (can be homemade or store-bought)
  • Filling (typical yaki-gyoza):
    Ground pork
    Cabbage (finely chopped and salted to draw out moisture)
    Garlic (grated)
    Ginger (grated)
    Nira (garlic chives) or green onions
    Soy sauce, sesame oil, sake, and pepper for seasoning
  • Dipping Sauce:
    Soy sauce, rice vinegar, and a few drops of chili oil (rayu)

How to Prepare Gyoza

  1. Step 1: Prepare the Filling
    1. Finely chop cabbage and sprinkle with salt. Let sit 10–15 minutes, then squeeze out excess moisture.
    2. Mix with ground pork, garlic, ginger, chives, soy sauce, sake, sesame oil, and pepper.
    3. Stir until the mixture becomes sticky — this binds everything together.
  2. Step 2: Wrap the Gyoza
    1. Place a small spoonful of filling in the center of a wrapper.
    2. Wet the edge of the wrapper with water.
    3. Fold in half and pleat the top edge while sealing the bottom — traditional gyoza have 5 pleats on one side.
      4. Tip: Don’t overfill, and press firmly to seal — no leaks allowed!
  3. Step 3: Pan-Fry the Gyoza (Yaki-Gyoza)
    1. Heat a bit of oil in a non-stick skillet over medium-high heat.
    2. Add gyoza flat-side down in a circular pattern.
    3. Cook for 2–3 minutes until the bottoms are golden brown.
  4. Step 4: Steam
    1. Add about 1/4 cup of water to the pan.
    2. Immediately cover with a lid to trap steam.
    3. Steam for about 4–5 minutes until water evaporates and the tops are cooked through.
  5. Step 5: Crisp the Bottom Again
    1. Once water is gone, uncover and cook for another 30–60 seconds to re-crisp the bottoms.
  6. Step 6: Serve
    1. Flip carefully onto a plate, browned side up and serve with dipping sauce!
