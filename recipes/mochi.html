Mochi

Description

Mochi dates back over 1,000 years to the Heian period (794–1185), originally used in Shinto rituals and offered to the gods for good fortune.
It became associated with the New Year (Oshōgatsu) and is still a staple in Japanese holiday celebrations. Mochi represents longevity and strength, and making it is as much a communal tradition as it is a culinary one — particularly through the ceremony of mochitsuki (pounding mochi).

Ingredients

Traditional mochi uses only a few pure ingredients:
  • Glutinous rice (Mochigome) – a special short-grain rice that becomes sticky when cooked
  • Water
  • (Optional) Sugar, cornstarch, or fillings like:
    Anko (sweet red bean paste)
    Kinako (roasted soybean flour)
    Fruit (like strawberries)
    Note: Store-bought “mochi flour” (Shiratamako or Mochiko) is often used for modern, quick versions.

Common Mochi Variations

  • Daifuku: Mochi stuffed with sweet red bean paste
  • Ichigo Daifuku: Red bean and strawberry inside
  • Yaki Mochi: Grilled mochi cakes, crispy outside
  • Ozoni: Mochi in savory New Year soup
  • Mochi ice cream: Sweet mochi filled with ice cream (a modern invention)

How to Prepare Traditional Mochi (with Steamed Rice)

  1. Step 1: Soak and Steam the Rice
    1. Rinse glutinous rice until water runs clear.
    2. Soak it in water for at least 8 hours or overnight.
    3. Steam the rice (not boil!) for 30–40 minutes until soft and sticky.
  2. Step 2: Pound the Rice (Mochitsuki)
    1. Transfer hot rice to a large wooden or stone mortar (usu).
    2. Use a heavy wooden mallet (kine) to pound the rice.
    3. One person wets and turns the rice. Another person pounds in a rhythm.
    4. Continue pounding until the rice turns into a smooth, stretchy dough.
      5. This is the heart of traditional mochi — a labor of love and teamwork.
  3. Step 3: Shape the Mochi
    1. Dust hands and surface with cornstarch or potato starch to prevent sticking.
    2. Pinch off small balls and shape while still warm.
    3. You can flatten and fill with anko or other fillings if desired.
